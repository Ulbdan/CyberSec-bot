1. What is cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity means protecting computers, phones, and data from hackers, viruses, and scams.

 2. Why is cybersecurity important?

Because it keeps your personal information safe and stops criminals from stealing or damaging it.

 3. What is a cyberattack?

A cyberattack is when someone tries to break into your system or steal your data.

 4. What is phishing?

Phishing is when attackers send fake emails or messages to trick you into giving personal information.

 5. How can I identify a phishing email?

Look for spelling mistakes, suspicious links, strange senders, or urgent messages asking you to click.

 6. What should I do if I clicked a phishing link?

Report it immediately, change your password, and inform your IT/security team.

 7. What is smishing?

Smishing is phishing through SMS/text messages.

 8. What is vishing?

Vishing is phishing done by voice calls where scammers try to trick you.

 9. What is malware?

Malware is harmful software that can steal or delete your data.

 10. What is a virus?

A virus is a type of malware that spreads from one device to another.

 11. What is ransomware?

Ransomware locks your files and asks you to pay money to get them back.

 12. What is spyware?

Spyware secretly watches what you do and steals your information.

 13. What is the best way to avoid malware?

Avoid unknown downloads, don’t click unsafe links, and use antivirus.

 14. What is a firewall?

A firewall acts like a security guard that blocks harmful traffic.

 15. What is a secure password?

A strong password uses letters, numbers, symbols, and is at least 12 characters long.

 16. Why should I not reuse passwords?

If one account is hacked, all other accounts become easy targets.

 17. What is a password manager?

A tool that stores and creates strong passwords for you.

 18. What is MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication)?

A security step that asks for more than just a password, like a code or fingerprint.

 19. Why is MFA important?

Even if someone knows your password, they still cannot enter your account.

 20. What should I do if my password is leaked?

Change it immediately and enable MFA.

 21. Why is public Wi-Fi dangerous?

Hackers on the same network can steal your information.

 22. How can I safely use public Wi-Fi?

Avoid banking, use a VPN, and never enter passwords on it.

 23. What is a VPN?

A tool that hides your internet activity and keeps it secure.

 24. What is encryption?

Encryption scrambles data so only the right person can read it.

 25. Why is encryption important?

It protects information even if hackers access it.

 26. What is a data breach?

A data breach happens when unauthorized people get access to private data.

 27. How can I know if my data was stolen?

You may get alerts from apps, companies, or find your email in breach-check websites.

 28. What should I do after a data breach?

Change passwords, enable MFA, and monitor accounts.

 29. What is social engineering?

Tricking people into giving information instead of hacking computers.

 30. Why is social engineering effective?

People are easier to fool than machines.

 31. What is shoulder surfing?

When someone watches your screen or keyboard secretly.

 32. What is tailgating in cybersecurity?

Someone following an employee into a restricted area without permission.

 33. What is safe browsing?

Avoiding risky websites and not clicking unsafe ads.

 34. What is HTTPS?

A secure website connection that protects your data.

 35. Why should I check for HTTPS?

It ensures the website encrypts your information.

 36. What is two-step verification?

Another word for MFA; it adds extra login security.

 37. What is identity theft?

When someone uses your personal information to pretend to be you.

 38. How can I protect my identity?

Use strong passwords, secure privacy settings, and avoid oversharing.

 39. What is privacy online?

Controlling who sees your personal information.

 40. Why should I care about privacy?

Hackers can misuse your data to steal money or impersonate you.

 41. What is GDPR?

A law that protects personal data in Europe.

 42. Why is GDPR important?

It ensures companies handle your data safely.

 43. What is ISO 27001?

A standard that helps companies manage information security.

 44. What is a knowledge base in cybersecurity?

A collection of security topics and answers that a chatbot uses.

 45. What is a cybersecurity culture?

A workplace where everyone follows safe security practices.

 46. What is safe data handling?

Using, storing, and sharing data correctly and securely.

 47. What is data minimization?

Collecting only the information you truly need.

 48. What is a strong security policy?

Rules that guide how employees protect company data.

 49. What is secure disposal?

Safely deleting or destroying sensitive data.

 50. What is BYOD (Bring Your Own Device)?

Using your personal device for work tasks.

 51. Why can BYOD be risky?

Personal devices may lack security tools.

 52. What is cloud security?

Protecting data stored in cloud services like Google Drive or OneDrive.

 53. Are cloud services safe?

Yes, if you use strong passwords and MFA.

 54. What is Zero Trust?

A security model that does not trust anyone automatically.

 55. Why is Zero Trust useful?

It reduces the chance of unauthorized access.

 56. What is incident reporting?

Telling the IT/security team when something suspicious happens.

 57. Why must incidents be reported quickly?

Faster reporting reduces damage.

 58. What is log monitoring?

Checking system activity to spot suspicious behavior.

 59. What is patching?

Updating software to fix security holes.

 60. Why are updates important?

They close vulnerabilities that hackers can exploit.

 61. What is brute-force attack?

Trying many password combinations until one works.

 62. How do I avoid brute-force attacks?

Use long passwords and enable MFA.

 63. What is a security token?

A device or app that gives verification codes.

 64. What is a digital footprint?

Everything you leave online—posts, logins, searches.

 65. Why should I reduce my digital footprint?

To prevent hackers from collecting information about you.

 66. What is a secure backup?

Storing a copy of data safely in case of loss.

 67. How often should backups be done?

Regularly—daily or weekly.

 68. What is two-way authentication?

Another term for MFA where two identity proofs are required.

 69. What is cybersecurity awareness?

Knowing how to stay safe online.

 70. Why are employees targeted in attacks?

People make mistakes, which attackers exploit.

 71. What is safe file sharing?

Using approved apps like OneDrive or Google Drive.

 72. What is unsafe file sharing?

Using personal email or untrusted apps to send work files.

 73. What is email spoofing?

When attackers fake someone else’s email address.

 74. What is DNS?

A system that converts website names into IP addresses.

 75. What is a DDoS attack?

A flood of traffic that makes a website crash.

 76. What is authentication?

Checking if a user is who they claim to be.

 77. What is authorization?

Deciding what a user is allowed to access.

 78. What is an access control list?

A list that shows who can access what.

 79. What is an antivirus?

Software that detects and removes malware.

 80. How does antivirus help?

It blocks and deletes harmful files.

 81. What is safe downloading?

Downloading only from trusted websites or stores.

 82. What is unsafe downloading?

Getting files from random websites or pop-ups.

 83. What is cyber hygiene?

Good daily habits like strong passwords and updates.

 84. What is a botnet?

A network of infected devices controlled by hackers.

 85. What is ethical hacking?

Authorized hacking to find and fix security problems.

 86. What is a vulnerability?

A weakness in a system that hackers can exploit.

 87. What is penetration testing?

Testing systems for weaknesses through simulated attacks.

 88. What is secure coding?

Writing software with safety in mind.

 89. What is encryption key?

A code used to lock and unlock encrypted data.

 90. Why should keys be protected?

Anyone with the key can access encrypted data.

 91. What is a cyber risk?

Anything that could cause harm to data or systems.

 92. What is a security audit?

A review of how well systems are protected.

 93. What is a threat actor?

A person or group that performs cyberattacks.

 94. What is MFA fatigue attack?

Hackers spam MFA requests hoping you approve one by mistake.

 95. What is safe password storage?

Using a password manager instead of notes or screenshots.

 96. What is a breach notification?

A message sent when a company’s data is stolen.

 97. What is a phishing simulation?

Fake phishing tests to train employees.

 98. What is account take-over?

When attackers control your account.

 99. How do I protect my accounts?

Use strong passwords + MFA and avoid reuse.

 100. What is the easiest way to stay safe online?

Think before you click, use strong passwords, and keep software updated.
